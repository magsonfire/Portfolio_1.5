About

My name is Maggie Cheung and I am a recent University of Toronto graduate seeking work in UX/UI design.

Merging Theory and Practise

I studied Psychology & Cognitive Science because I wanted to figure out what makes people tick. How can the same song make one person cry and another laugh? How do humans make decisions and solve problems? How does form afford function, in a mind, in a machine, or through a digital interface?

My passion for digital design began as a pastime of editing my father's websites. It grew into a thirst for creating and critiquing my own interfaces, and since then I've accrued a decade of familiarity working with web interfaces. More recently, I have spent the past three years helping my clients achieve their goals by creating digital and print experiences that are fluent, fresh, and immediately relevant.

Solving People-Centric Problems

How do you get users to click here, instead of there? How can a colour palette or banner placement change one's bottom line? Why don't users understand how to interact with a certain interface? By combining my academic background with practical design experience, I design digital interfaces targeted to solve people-centric problems just like these.

Soft Skills

Experiences in nonprofit management and fundraising have helped me hone soft skills in negotiation and project management, while a love of theatre and poetry have bootstrapped my public speaking and copywriting abilities.

Now, I'm looking for my next adventure; I'm looking for people-centred problems and a team that enjoys solving them.
