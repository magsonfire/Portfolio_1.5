Lecture 1 - Culture, Difference, Prejudice

Study Notes

(click) Study Notes

Study Questions

  1. How does culture and language make homo sapiens particularly adaptable?
  2. Explain how the four fields of classic anthropology arose in the imperialist context.
  3. Difference in itself does not cause prejudice. Then where does prejudice against people who are different come from?
  4. What is an "invented" category such as "race"? In what sense do such categories exist (are real)?

Lecture 2 - Signifying Reality

Study Notes

(click) Study Notes

Study Questions

  1. Explain what is meant by "naturalization," and give an example other than "race."
  2. Saussure taught that the sign consists of the signifier plus the signified. In ordinary language, however, the word "sign" may refer to just the signifier. Why is it important to recognize that the signified must also be included? (Hint: consider what was said in class about the role of the context.)
  3. What is the difference between an icon, an index, and a symbol? Improve your understanding by randomly choosing visual, auditory, or other signs, including words (remember that words are signs!), and then asking yourself which of the three they are.
  4. Randomly choose some ads and identify their connotation and denotation. What is the advantage, to advertisers, of relying on denotation more than on connotation?

Lecture 3 - Constructing Reality

Study Notes

(click) Study Notes

Study Questions

  1. Relate the so-called Whorf Hypothesis to the way color and other discontinuous features of the world are described in different languages.
  2. What, in Lacan's terms, is the difference between the real and reality?
  3. Explain the stages of infant and child development according to Lacan, and the role that different kinds of signs (the icon, the symbol) and sign systems (images, language) play in it.
  4. What is meant by "the linguistic construction of the self"?

Lecture 4 - Religion

Study Notes

(click) Study Notes

Study Questions

  1. Religion finds signs of the Real in reality. Explain, using the terms "the Real" and "reality" in Lacan's sense, as explained in the lecture.
  2. What would be your example if you were to argue like Bruno Latour, that the world is still enchanted today?
  3. Give an example (such as the "lulu" spirits on Mayotte) of how religion links culture to what is outside it.
  4. How does religion support the social order (meaning: arrangements in society including power relations)?

Lecture 5 - (National) Identity: Imagined Community. The Nation. Transnational Influences

Study Notes

(click) Study Notes

Study Questions

  1. All nations are imagined. Explain with Canada as an example, and give an example or two of how Canadian identity is performed.
  2. Give examples of nations that were an imagined community before they had a state.
  3. Use the example of a) Russian and Ukrainian or Hindi and Urdu and b) Chinese, to show that the distinction between "language" and "dialect" is political and not linguistic.
  4. Explain how tourists changed Panamanian identity by their partiality to Afro-Antillean music.

Lecture 6 - Our World and Their World

Study Notes

(click) Study Notes

Study Questions

  1. Is moral relativism the opposite of cultural relativity? What is the difference between the two?
  2. What is the difference between the way a) English and b) Hindi treat the sounds ph (aspirated p) and p= (unaspirated p)?
  3. Explain the difference between emic and etic descriptions of what the anthropologist finds in "the field."
  4. We think of the political economy of emotions when we think of Brazilian mothers in the poorest of city neighborhoods abandoning sickly babies to death. Explain.

Lecture 7 - Relatedness

Study Notes

(click) Study Notes

Study Questions

  1. Native speakers of Norwegian who had almost no exposure to English as children, have a fighting chance to learn English without a foreign accent when they are adults. So why can native speakers of Spanish, Polish, or Chinese (who also had very little exposure to English as children) seldom if ever accomplish such a feat?
  2. In matrilineal societies, your mother's brother can have more decision-making power over you than your father or your father's brother. Explain the logic behind this.
  3. What are the main differences between the so-called Hawaiian, Sudanese, and Eskimo kinship systems?
  4. How do commensality and milk-siblingship add a flexible, social and cultural dimension to kinship in Pulau Langkawi?

Lecture 8 - Economics: The Base of Culture

Study Notes

(click) Study Notes

Study Questions

  1. What are some examples of the a) means and b) relations of production in the economy?
  2. What is "revised" in the revised model discussed in class, of the relationship between base and superstructure?
  3. Describe the economic base in a) foraging, b) horticulturalism, c) pastoralism, and d) intensive agriculture.
  4. Describe some of the typical superstructure in a) foraging, b) horticulturalism, c) pastoralism, and d) intensive agriculture.

Lecture 9 - Post-Industrialism and the Postcolony

Study Notes

(click) Study Notes

Study Questions

  1. What is the most typical form of production in a) industrial and b) postindustrial society?
  2. What are the typical features of a) industrial and b) postindustrial superstructure?
  3. What is neoliberalism and how would it be employed to decrease the unequal distribution of global wealth?
  4. Who are the Asian Tigers?

Lecture 10 - Getting Consent: Hegemony and Governmentality

Study Notes

(click) Study Notes

Study Questions

  1. What are the pros and cons of microfinance?
  2. Working class people use more non-standard language. However, on formal occasions their language becomes more standard - their formal "register" is more like that of middle class people. How is this an example of what Gramsci meant by prestige as a factor in hegemony?
  3. Knowingly or not, we all give our consent to hegemony. Explain, with reference to what Gramsci meant by "spontaneous" consent.
  4. How is the Panopticon an example of governmentality?

Lecture 11 - Change in an Age of Online Media: Dissent and Incorporation

Study Notes

(click) Study Notes

Study Questions

  1. What did Marshall McLuhan mean by "the medium is the message"?
  2. Give examples of how the way people consume news via the internet fragments the public, while the way people previously consumed news via television newscasts united the public.
  3. Explain, with examples, what is meant by the proposition that the relationship between hip hop and the hegemony was once mainly resistance, but now mainly incorporation?
  4. What did Antonio Negri mean by suggesting that any resistance may just become the next stage in the hegemony?
